## Choose a direction

- **Variant A — Structural**
  - Best for: first-time readers who need the pieces named plainly.
  - Strongest on: what the parts are and the order of operations.
  - Weakest on: exact magnitude and sign.

- **Variant B — Quantitative**
  - Best for: readers who want to see the score get built numerically.
  - Strongest on: sign, magnitude, contribution, and total score.
  - Weakest on: the “same weights, moved threshold” intuition.

- **Variant C — Dynamic**
  - Best for: readers confused specifically about what the bias changes.
  - Strongest on: fixed weights with a sliding threshold / boundary.
  - Weakest on: how the score itself is assembled.

My default recommendation is a **hybrid of B + C**:

- use **B** as the main static article image
- keep **C** as the companion animation
- borrow the plain labeling discipline from **A**
